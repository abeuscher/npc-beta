# 362. Guided Tour Redesign — Three Focused Single-Area Tours (Dashboard, CRM, CMS)

Retire the session-338 multi-page product walkthrough — whose cross-page hard-navigation is the "non-starter" that made the tour feel broken — and replace it with three short, self-contained tours that keep the guided-tour premise: (A) a **Dashboard overview** that stays on the dashboard and highlights the main sidebar areas, (B) a **CRM deep-dive**, and (C) a **CMS deep-dive**.

## What is being retired (session 338, as modified through 361)

The current guided tour is a **single multi-page walkthrough**:

- `resources/js/admin/tour/tours/product-tour.js` — an 11-step definition that hard-navigates across the whole admin (Welcome → Contacts → a contact record → Transactions → Mailing lists → Events → Importer → Roles → help). **This is the thing being removed.**
- `controller.js` — the multi-page engine: drives one contiguous page-group per driver instance, persists the active step to `localStorage` (`state.js`), hard-navigates at page boundaries, resumes on load. **The cross-page machinery is what the owner is rejecting** — it goes (or is drastically reduced) depending on the navigation model chosen for B/C.
- `AdminPanelProvider` `HEAD_END` role-gated `window.__npTour` URL map; the demo-safe `Tour{Roles,Import}ShowcasePage` stand-ins. **Keep only what the new tours actually visit.**
- `tests/e2e/tour/product-tour.spec.ts` — the standing spec for the 11-step walk. **Replaced** by per-tour specs.

Removal must be clean: no dead render hooks, no orphaned showcase pages/routes, no `data-tour` markers left injected for anchors no tour uses.

## What is kept (reusable primitives — do not rebuild)

- **driver.js** + the thin wrapper around it (`driver().highlight()` / `drive()`), the `np-tour-popover` styling.
- **Owned `data-tour` anchor convention** (never Filament `.fi-*` classes) and `anchors.js` — `resolveAnchor`, `waitForElement`, and the session-361 **`waitForStableRect`** (settle-before-position) primitive.
- The **`data-np-tour-start` launcher** delegation pattern (`index.js`).
- **Eager Memberships relation-manager** (`MembershipsRelationManager::$isLazy = false`, session 361) — the general fix for lazy Filament components loading late and shifting layout. If a CRM-tour step anchors on any other lazy relation-manager/widget, apply the same eager treatment or anchor on stable content.
- The **session-361 lazy-load lesson** (see that log): a tour anchor must resolve to content that is *in the DOM and geometrically settled* when the step fires. Lazy relation-managers, deferred widgets, and post-hydration growth are the enemy. Prefer eager, high-on-the-page, stable anchors.

## Pitfalls from session 361 — do not repeat (read the 361 log first)

Session 361 burned real time fighting symptoms before the owner reframed the problem. Start from these so the rebuild doesn't re-derive them:

- **Fix the anchor, don't chase the layout.** The membership step landed off-screen because its target — a **lazy** relation-manager — loaded late and shifted the page after driver had already positioned. The wrong fix (built and discarded in 361): a polling "watcher" that re-scrolled the element into view; it fought fast advances and was fragile. The right fix: **make the target eager (`$isLazy = false`) and anchor high on stable content.** If a 362 step's target is lazy / late-loading, make it eager or pick a different anchor — do **not** add a reactive scroll-chaser.
- **A bar/popover overlap is a spatial problem, not a z-index one.** If 362 keeps a persistent bar: it collides with driver.js popovers only where they occupy the same screen space, and **escalating z-index is a dead-end** (361 went as far as `1e9+1` before the owner flagged it). The working resolution: bar z-index **just above driver's overlay (`10000`) but below its popover (`1000000000`)**, so the popover's own buttons win where they overlap and the bar is the visible fallback elsewhere. Better: re-decide whether a bottom bar is needed at all once framing is reliable.
- **Verify against realistic data, not the bare e2e hero.** The bug did **not** reproduce on the bare `tour.hero@nphelper.demo` contact; it took a contact *with* a membership (rich, multi-stage-loading content) to surface. Seed realistic data for each tour's browser verification.
- **Do NOT undo the durable keepers while removing the walkthrough:** the **2FA e2e auth fix** (`tests/e2e/helpers/db.ts` + `auth.ts` — the entire admin Playwright suite depends on it) and the **eager `MembershipsRelationManager`** (a general no-layout-jump win). Remove the *tour*; keep these.

- **Tour A — Dashboard Overview.** Stays on `/admin`. Highlights each **sidebar nav group** (CRM, CMS, Finance, Tools, Settings) and likely the topbar affordances (View site, help). No navigation. Purpose: orient a cold prospect to the major areas of the product in one place.
- **Tour B — CRM deep-dive.** "Drills into" the CRM (contacts, organizations, mailing lists, memberships).
- **Tour C — CMS deep-dive.** "Drills into" the CMS (pages, blog, events, navigation/theme, media).

**The step set and copy for all three come from the owner's outlines/scripts** (see open question 2 — provided vs authored in-session). Do not invent the flow; the owner drives content, the session drives the anchoring/mechanics/verification.

## Open questions to resolve at session start *(settle at the Open Gate — do not commit-and-unwind)*

1. **Navigation model for B & C (load-bearing).** The rejected behavior is a single tour *skipping across many pages*. Options for each area tour: (a) **single-page** — the tour stays on one landing page and highlights features there (no hard nav); (b) **contained navigation** — it drills into a couple of related pages but transitions smoothly and stays within the area. **Recommended lean:** default each area tour to **single-page / minimal-navigation**, launched from within its area so it *starts* on the right page and stays; only introduce a hop if the owner's script demands it, and if so handle it far more gracefully than 338 did (or not at all). Confirm per tour — this decides how much of `controller.js`/`state.js` survives.
2. **Source of the three tour scripts.** The owner said "I have … 1 script … then 2." Are these **actual files/content to integrate**, or an **outline the session authors into step definitions**? Confirm the source of truth for steps + copy before building; bring a storyboard for copy approval either way.
3. **Launchers.** Three tours now, not one. How is each started? (e.g. the dashboard "Tour the Product" button → Tour A, which ends by pointing at the CRM/CMS nav; Tours B/C launched from the CRM/CMS landing pages, or from a small "Take a tour" menu.) Confirm.
4. **Persistent control bar (built in 361).** Keep it for the new tours or drop it? Single-page scope + the framing fix sharply reduce the stranding risk the bar was built for. Confirm keep / rework / drop.
5. **Multi-page engine + resume.** If the tours are single-page (Q1), remove the page-group driver, `state.js` localStorage resume, and the `__npTour` URL map — keep only single-page driving + anchor resolution. Confirm the simplification scope so the retirement is clean, not half-gutted.
6. **Role-gating / demo-safe showcases.** Tour A highlights nav groups a restricted (demo) role may not reach; B/C may point at role-gated features. Keep role-aware step-skipping (and the showcases) or drop them? Confirm.
7. **Relationship to the post-Beta contextual-tours stub.** Does this redesign become the foundation for the per-view, help-launched contextual tours, or stay demo-marketing-only? Confirm whether to update that `session-outlines.md` stub.

## Phases

Remove `product-tour.js` and the multi-page machinery the new model doesn't need (per Q1/Q5); refactor `anchors.js`/`index.js`/the driver wrapper into a clean **single-area tour engine** that can run any one of the three tours by id; wire the launcher(s) (Q3); delete orphaned showcases/routes/hooks/markers.
