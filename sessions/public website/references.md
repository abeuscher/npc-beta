# Reference Images Index

The four images in `references/` are structural specs, not surface inspiration. They tell the agent what shape a section should take. They do not tell the agent what colors, fonts, or illustrations to use. The voice and visual restraint of NonprofitCRM is the opposite of these reference sites — what is being borrowed is the *page architecture*, not the *vibe*.

## reference-kitchen-sink-saas-page.png

A full-page capture of an enterprise B2B SaaS marketing page (Adobe Marketo). Look at it for:

- Section ordering and rhythm (hero → intro → feature explainer → use cases → social proof → secondary features → CTA → form)
- The principle of one idea per band, each band visually distinct via background tone
- Hero structure: large headline left, supporting visual right, two CTAs below the body
- The 2x2 use-case grid pattern with icon + heading + body per cell
- The final-CTA band as a clear conversion moment, set off by a different background

Do *not* take from it: the dense copy, the marketing adjectives, the abstract illustrations, the stock photography of executives.

## reference-section-band-cycling.png

A full-page capture of Clay's homepage. Look at it for:

- The background-tone cycling rhythm (white → tinted → white → dark → white)
- How a single visual band reads as one chapter regardless of what's inside it
- The "real product screenshot inside a soft container" pattern for showing the product
- Restrained type scale — headings are large but not enormous

Do *not* take from it: the custom 3D illustration style, the playful color saturation, the GTM-jargon copy.

## reference-restrained-crm-page.png

A full-page capture of Attio's homepage. Look at it for:

- The cleaner, less-decorated end of the spectrum — closer to where NonprofitCRM should land
- Minimal hero with the product screenshot as the only visual element
- Generous whitespace between sections
- The "we are serious software" tone communicated through restraint rather than decoration
- The structure is still kitchen-sink, but the surface is quiet

This is the closest visual match to where NonprofitCRM should end up. When in doubt about how much decoration to use in a section, err toward this reference, not the other two.

## reference-feature-grid-pattern.png

A full-page capture of Gong's homepage. Look at it for:

- The hero "see it in action" sub-band — a product visual immediately below the headline that gives the page somewhere to land
- The feature grid pattern (six cells, each a small icon + heading + short body)
- The customer logo row + stat row pattern (skip this one — NonprofitCRM has no customers yet to display, and inventing them would be dishonest)
- The "everyone wins with [product]" persona-targeted band — interesting structurally but probably not needed for NonprofitCRM's audience

Do *not* take from it: the bright marketing color palette, the heavy illustration of "AI agents," the persona-grid pattern unless it earns its place.

## Cross-reference cheat sheet

When the agent needs to decide what a section should look like:

- **Hero pattern** → reference-restrained-crm-page (cleanest), reference-kitchen-sink-saas-page (most structural)
- **Feature grid (3 or 4 columns)** → reference-feature-grid-pattern, reference-kitchen-sink-saas-page
- **Band rhythm and background tone cycling** → reference-section-band-cycling
- **Final CTA section shape** → reference-kitchen-sink-saas-page
- **How much whitespace is enough** → reference-restrained-crm-page

When the structural moves between two references conflict, prefer the restrained one.
