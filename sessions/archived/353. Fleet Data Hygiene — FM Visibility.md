## Open questions to resolve at session start

1. **Subcheck `value` shape.** A single total cruft count, or the four-category breakdown object (`orphan_event_pages` / `scrub_records` / `orphan_media_dirs` / `dead_owner_media`)? FM-side UX wants enough to display a useful per-node line; the breakdown is richer and still non-PII. Settle the shape and the `threshold` field.
2. **Status semantics.** Does `data_hygiene` roll into the **worst-of overall status** (`overallStatus()`), or is it **informational** (its status reflects the counts but a cruft pile, however large, is not a `red`-class health failure)? Lean informational — at most a generous soft `yellow` so a heavily-crufted node surfaces for attention, **never `red`** — but confirm at the gate. Whatever is chosen, document the worst-of interaction in the contract spec.
3. **Cost on a polled endpoint.** `counts()` walks the filesystem (`orphanMediaDirectories()`) and scans all media (`deadOwnerMedia()`). `/api/health` is polled often. Decide: cache with a short TTL, compute on a schedule and read the cached value (mind the scheduler-runner gap — no `schedule:work` on the worker), split the cheap DB-only counts from the expensive filesystem walk, or accept the cost. Pick the strategy and justify it.
4. **FM-side handoff scope.** Confirm the FM-side toggle + subcheck consumption is fully out of CRM scope and is captured in the cross-repo block + a handoff note (mirror the demo-node handoff pattern), so the FM repo can absorb v2.4.0.

## Drift signals (stop and surface)

- The chosen `value` shape would put anything but aggregate counts on the wire — stop; the boundary forbids it.
- `counts()` is too expensive to call per-poll and no caching strategy is clean without the scheduler runner — surface the cost/architecture before wiring a heavy path into `/api/health`.
- Making `data_hygiene` affect the worst-of overall status would flip real nodes to `yellow`/`red` for benign cruft — surface the UX implication before changing node health semantics.
